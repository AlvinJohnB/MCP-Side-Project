Baguio

Summer Capital of the Philippines

Nestled in the Cordillera mountains, Baguio offers a cool climate year-round, a welcome escape from the tropical heat. Pine trees, foggy mornings, and scenic views give the city a romantic, laid-back feel. Known for Burnham Park, Mines View Park, and the Baguio Night Market, it blends natural beauty with vibrant local culture. It’s also home to artists, students, and a growing food scene.

Boracay

White Sands and Island Vibes

Famous for its powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters, Boracay’s White Beach is often ranked among the world’s best. It’s the quintessential tropical paradise—perfect for swimming, partying, sunset sailing, and beach lounging. After a period of rehabilitation, the island now balances tourism with sustainability, offering a cleaner, calmer, and more regulated experience

Cebu

The Queen City of the South

Cebu is a dynamic mix of history, city life, and natural beauty. It’s where Magellan first set foot, and where you’ll find Magellan’s Cross, Basilica del Santo Niño, and Spanish colonial landmarks. Beyond the city, you’ll discover Kawasan Falls, Osmeña Peak, and world-class diving in Moalboal and Malapascua. Cebu offers something for every kind of traveler—from heritage tours to adventure escapades.

Manila

The Heartbeat of the Nation

The capital and cultural melting pot of the Philippines, Manila is chaotic, colorful, and full of contrasts. Explore the walled city of Intramuros, the historic Rizal Park, and vibrant districts like Binondo, the world’s oldest Chinatown. Manila is the country’s center for politics, commerce, education, and pop culture—constantly evolving, always buzzing.

Palawan

Nature’s Last Frontier

Palawan is postcard-perfect—turquoise waters, towering limestone cliffs, and hidden lagoons. El Nido and Coron are famed for dramatic island-hopping tours and world-class diving. Meanwhile, the Puerto Princesa Underground River is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Untouched jungles and marine sanctuaries make Palawan a paradise for nature lovers and eco-travelers.

Siquijor

The Island of Mystique

Often called the “Island of Fire,” Siquijor is steeped in mysticism, folklore, and natural beauty. It’s known for healing traditions, quiet beaches, waterfalls like Cambugahay Falls, and old Spanish-era churches. Unlike more crowded destinations, Siquijor is serene and soul-soothing, making it ideal for off-the-beaten-path explorers and spiritual seekers.
